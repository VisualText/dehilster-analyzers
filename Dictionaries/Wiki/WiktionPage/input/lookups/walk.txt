===Etymology 1===
{{root|en|ine-pro|*welH-}}
From {{inh|en|enm|walken|t=to move, roll, turn, revolve, toss}}, from {{inh|en|ang|wealcan|t=to move round, revolve, roll, turn, toss}}, {{m|ang|ġewealcan|t=to go, traverse}}; and {{inh|en|enm|walkien|t=to roll, stamp, walk, wallow}}, from {{inh|en|ang|wealcian|t=to curl, roll up}}; both from {{inh|en|gmw-pro|*walkan}}, from {{inh|en|gem-pro|*walkaną}}, {{m|gem-pro|*walkōną|t=to twist, turn, roll about, full}}, from {{der|en|ine-pro|*walg-|t=to twist, turn, move}}. Cognate with {{cog|sco|walk|t=to walk}}, {{cog|stq|walkje|t=to full; drum; flex; mill}}, {{cog|fy|swalkje|t=to wander, roam}}, {{cog|nl|walken|t=to full, work hair or felt}}, {{cog|nl|zwalken|t=to wander about}}, {{cog|de|walken|t=to flex, full, mill, drum}}, {{cog|da|valke|t=to waulk, full}}, {{cog|la|valgus|t=bandy-legged, bow-legged}}, {{cog|sa|वल्गति||amble, bound, leap, dance}}. More at {{l|en|vagrant}} and {{l|en|whelk}}. Doublet of {{l|en|waulk}}.
