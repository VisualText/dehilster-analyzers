The old fisherman rowed his small wooden boat across the calm gray harbor before the morning sun rose over the distant hills. He had spent many quiet years on these cold waters, and he knew every hidden rock beneath the restless surface. When the heavy fog finally lifted, the bright sails of the other boats appeared along the rocky shore. His weathered hands pulled the wet ropes with a slow and steady rhythm that he had learned as a young boy. By the time the village market opened, he had filled three large baskets with fresh silver fish.
