On the first cold morning of the new term, the nervous students hurried across the wide stone courtyard toward their classrooms. The strict old teacher, who had taught mathematics for thirty years, wrote a difficult problem on the dusty board. Although many pupils struggled at first, a quiet girl in the back row quickly found an elegant solution. During the long afternoon, the class discussed ancient history and read aloud from a thick worn book. When the final bell rang, the excited children rushed outside and scattered across the muddy playing field.
