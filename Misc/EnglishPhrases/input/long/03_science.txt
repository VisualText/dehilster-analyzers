When a bright star runs out of its nuclear fuel, the enormous pressure at its core can no longer support the outer layers. The collapsing material rushes inward at tremendous speed, and a violent explosion scatters heavy elements across the cold emptiness of space. These drifting clouds of gas and dust slowly gather under their own gravity over millions of long years. Eventually new stars and orbiting planets form within the swirling disks that surround them. Because this process repeats again and again, the universe steadily builds the rich chemistry that life later requires.
