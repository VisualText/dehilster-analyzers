The ancient city, which had survived countless wars and long droughts, slowly grew along the muddy banks of the wide river. Merchants from distant lands brought rare spices, bright fabrics, and strange new ideas to its crowded markets. Although the powerful kings built tall stone walls around the inner palace, the busy streets outside remained noisy and free. Over many centuries the people developed a rich culture that blended music, poetry, and careful craftsmanship. Today the ruins of those proud buildings still attract curious travelers from every corner of the modern world.
